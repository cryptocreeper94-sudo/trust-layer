9. WORDS AS SEALS, NOT TOOLS
============================

The Goal:
- Find terminology that by its structure exposes manipulation
- Self-referential integrity
- If someone corrupts it, the corruption becomes linguistically visible

Candidates Discussed:
- "Proof" - carries its own standard, but can be "disproved" with mere doubt
- "Record" - immutable by definition, falsification exposes itself
- "Verified" - already checked
- "Sealed" - closed, final
- "Witnessed" - multiple parties confirmed

- Semantically neutral - doesn't carry emotional baggage like "coin" or "token"
- Hard to weaponize - "Signal is a scam" sounds absurd
- Inherently meaningful - a transmission, proof of presence, a beacon
- Flexible - singular or plural, noun or verb, without distortion
- Philosophically aligned - implies intent, clarity, frequency, truth
- "Signal isn't what you buy. It's what you earn by showing up in truth."

The Pulse-Signal Connection:
- "Pulse" is already part of the Trust Layer architecture
- A pulse is a transmission, a heartbeat, a rhythm of truth
- The Signal is not a currency - it's a transmission of verified intent
- You can't hype a signal. You either receive it or you don't.

The Asymmetry Problem:
- Proof requires the positive assertion with evidence
- Disproof only requires injecting doubt
- Good things get destroyed, bad things persist
- The burden is always on truth-tellers
