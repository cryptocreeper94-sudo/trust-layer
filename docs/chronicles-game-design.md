# DARKWAVE CHRONICLES - Game Design Document
*Living Document - Last Updated: January 7, 2026*

## CORE PHILOSOPHY

### The Foundation
- **No End State** - This is not a game you "beat." It's a persistent, ever-evolving parallel life.
- **Real-Time 1:1** - 1 hour real time = 1 hour game time. 24-hour day cycles match reality.
- **Persistent World** - The world runs 24/7. When you're offline, other players and events continue.
- **The Mirror** - Your character is a reflection of you, shaped by your choices, personality, and voice.
- **Constant Progression** - Always something to learn, achieve, discover. No ceiling. Ever.

### What Makes This Different
- Not a game you grind for a weekend - it's a life you live alongside
- Real time investment = real emotional connection
- The journey matters as much as the destination
- Your parallel self develops over months and years, not hours

## NARRATIVE FRAMEWORK

### The Hook (First Experience)

**You start in PRESENT DAY.**

- You wake up in your life - you have a home, basic necessities, everyday comforts
- It feels like you've been living this life already (even though you just started)
- You go about your day - normal activities, routines, maybe meet people

**Then something happens.**

Within the first day or few days of play, a triggering event occurs:
- Could be something you do, something you find, or completely random
- Suddenly you're ZAPPED - pulled away against your will
- You land in another dimension/era - not one you chose
- You're disoriented, confused, desperate to get back

**The Desperate Return**

- You have to figure out how to get home
- This is your first real quest - survival and escape
- Through gameplay, you find a way back to present day

**The Realization**

- You're back home, safe... but shaken
- What you thought was science fiction is REAL
- There are other dimensions, other eras, and somehow you can access them
- The veil has dropped - you've seen behind reality

**The Journey Begins**

- Curiosity and necessity pull you back
- You start finding cryptic clues about what happened
- You discover pieces of a greater puzzle - the Sigils
- Over time, you learn to control the leaps instead of being pulled randomly
- Eventually you master the Portal and can go where you CHOOSE

### Terminology Consideration

Consider calling it:
- **Quantum Leaping** instead of "time travel"
- **Dimensions** instead of "eras" (each dimension exists in a different time period)
- **The Veil** - what separates dimensions (and it's thinning/broken)
- **Chrono-Echoes** - your parallel selves across dimensions

This frames it as interdimensional travel, not just hopping through history.

### Relationships & Life Partners

Your parallel self can form deep connections:
- **Allies** - Political/strategic partnerships
- **Friends** - Companionship and loyalty
- **Life Partners** - Romantic relationships (implied intimacy, not explicit)
  - Can live together, build a life
  - Affects gameplay - shared resources, emotional stakes
  - Can leave or be left - real relationship dynamics
  - Partner may or may not be able to quantum leap with you

Relationships add emotional weight and consequences to your choices.

### Story Arc Summary

1. **ACT 1: The Awakening** - Normal life → First involuntary leap → Desperate return
2. **ACT 2: The Discovery** - Learning the truth → Finding first clues → First intentional leap
3. **ACT 3: The Hunt** - Collecting Sigils → Mastering travel → Uncovering the mystery
4. **ACT 4: The Purpose** - Understanding why YOU → The greater threat → The ultimate choice
5. **ONGOING: The Life** - Never ends → Always more to discover → Your parallel self forever

## PROGRESSION SYSTEM

### Era Ladder (70+ Eras)
- Each era has ONE main "Chronicle Quest"
- Complete the Chronicle Quest → Earn a Chrono Keystone → Unlock next era
- Eras vary in difficulty:
  - Some are accessible (complete in days)
  - Some are brutal (weeks of real-time effort, harder challenges)
  - Difficulty = prestige and better rewards
- Some eras require more difficult quests as "special" hard-to-reach time periods

### Quest Tiers
| Type | Duration | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Quick Tasks | 1 hour or less | Find an item in town |
| Day Missions | 1-3 days | Escort a merchant, investigate ruins |
| Chronicle Quests | 1-2 weeks | Main era quest to unlock next era |
| Saga Arcs | Months | Multi-era storylines leading to Sigils |

### The 7 Sigils (Ultimate Mastery)
- Earned through completing thematic saga arcs spanning multiple eras
- NOT required for basic era travel
- Each Sigil grants special time-manipulation abilities
- All 7 = Chronomancer status (ultimate mastery, free travel anywhere)

**The Seven Sigils:**
1. Diplomacy - Alliances & negotiation
2. Exploration - Discovery of hidden places
3. Innovation - Advancing technology through ages
4. Faith - Spiritual quests and belief systems
5. Rebellion - Revolution and overthrowing tyrants
6. Stewardship - Leadership and civilization building
7. Transcendence - The final awakening

## GAMEPLAY LOOP

### Check-In Sessions (2-20 minutes typical)
1. **Catch-Up Digest** - What happened in the world while you were away
2. **Decision Phase** - Choose actions, advance quests, interact with NPCs
3. **Action Execution** - Complete tasks, encounters, conversations
4. **Exit Planning** - Prepare for going offline (return home or set up camp)

### Activity Layers (Always Something To Do)
- **Daily Living** - Town tasks, relationships, economy management
- **Side Missions** - 5-30 min tactical encounters for resources
- **Chronicle Quest** - Multi-day main progression toward next era
- **Exploration** - Discover hidden locations, artifacts, secrets
- **Social** - Interact with other players, form alliances, trade

## OFFLINE BEHAVIOR

### When You Log Off
**Your character does NOT continue acting autonomously.**

Your parallel self enters "hibernation mode":
- No walking around making decisions without you
- No interacting with other players or NPCs
- Not an active presence others can encounter

### Two Options When Logging Off Mid-Mission:

**Option A: Return Home**
- Log off → Character returns to their domain/home
- Log back in → "You wake up at home"
- Travel back to continue mission where you left off
- Time passed in the world, but you weren't actively participating

**Option B: Camp In Place (If Prepared)**
- If you have proper gear (tent, shelter, supplies) → Set up camp and wait
- If NOT prepared → Forced to return home
- Rewards planning and preparation for long expeditions

### What DOES Continue:
- The world clock keeps ticking (day becomes night, seasons change)
- Other players continue their activities
- World events unfold
- NPC lives continue
- But YOUR character is resting, not participating

## ENCOUNTER SYSTEM

### Turn-Based Encounters (Inspired by Final Fantasy 7)
- NOT real-time action combat
- Strategic, thoughtful decision-making
- Animated characters with movement and actions (not static images)

### Encounter Types:
- **Combat** - Physical confrontation with enemies
- **Diplomacy** - Negotiation, persuasion, deal-making
- **Puzzle** - Problem-solving challenges
- **Social** - Relationship building, reputation management
- **Discovery** - Finding artifacts, clues, hidden knowledge

---

## SURVIVAL & REALISM

### Extended Quest Requirements
When on missions lasting more than a day:
- **Shelter Required** - Can't sleep in the open
  - Stone Age: Caves, primitive shelters
  - Medieval: Inns, camps, tents
  - Future: Space pods, bio-domes, stations
- **Era-Appropriate Gear** - Equipment must match the time period
- **Planning Matters** - Prepare supplies before long expeditions
- **Consequences** - Unprepared = forced to return home

### The Parallel Self Basics
- Sleeps when you're offline (not wandering)
- Needs rest, food, shelter (abstracted but present)
- Can't be left "standing in the middle of the road"

## COLLABORATION SYSTEM

### Asynchronous Cooperation
- Contribute to shared quests without being online together
- Deposit resources, research, items toward group goals
- Progress accumulates from all contributors

### Synchronous Cooperation
- Schedule "party windows" for real-time co-op sessions
- Joint decision-making on critical quest moments
- Shared encounters and battles

### Team Benefits
- Faster quest completion with more contributors
- Shared rewards distributed fairly
- Tackle harder content together

### Failure Protection
- If a teammate goes inactive for 12+ hours, NPC steward fills role
- Reduced efficiency but mission doesn't fail
- Prevents one player from blocking group progress

## NAVIGATION & PRESENTATION

### Visual Style Goals
- **NOT static images** - Animated, living scenes with movement
- **Inspired by**: Final Fantasy 7 (world map, explorable towns, turn-based)
- **Also inspired by**: Clash of Clans (overhead settlement view, tap buildings)

### Technical Approach
- 2.5D isometric or animated scene-based (web/mobile compatible)
- Characters move, environments have ambient animation
- Weather, day/night cycles, crowds, smoke, fire - the world feels alive

### Navigation Flow
- **World Map** - Overview of regions/eras, tap to travel
- **Settlement View** - Overhead view of town/city, tap buildings to enter
- **Interior Scenes** - Shops, homes, dungeons - interact with contents
- **Wilderness** - Exploration areas between settlements

## THE PORTAL / TIME TRAVEL

### Keystones (Era Access)
- One Keystone per era
- Earned by completing that era's Chronicle Quest
- Each Keystone unlocks the next era in sequence

### Sigils (Mastery Abilities)
- 7 total, earned through saga arcs spanning multiple eras
- Grant special time-manipulation powers
- NOT required for basic era-to-era travel

### Chronomancer Status (All 7 Sigils)
- Complete freedom of movement through all eras
- Ultimate mastery of time travel
- Unlocks endgame content and abilities

## LONG-TERM ENGAGEMENT

### After Unlocking All 70 Eras
The game never ends. Content continues through:

1. **Era Remixes** - Alternate history versions (What if Rome never fell?)
2. **World Events** - Community-wide challenges affecting all players
3. **Prestige Tiers** - Reset select progress for legacy rewards
4. **Procedural Expeditions** - AI-generated missions for infinite variety
5. **Player-Created Content** - Design your own quests (future feature)
6. **New Eras Added** - Continuous development expands the universe
7. **Difficulty Tiers** - Harder versions of existing content
8. **Seasonal Events** - Real-world holiday tie-ins

## THE PARALLEL SELF

### More Than A Character
- Reflects your personality through responses and choices
- Uses your voice (voice cloning technology)
- Develops and grows over months/years of play
- A true mirror of who you are (or who you want to be)

### Personality System (To Be Developed)
- Choices shape personality traits
- Traits affect dialogue options and NPC reactions
- Your parallel self becomes unique to you

## MVP SCOPE (Phase 1 - Prove The Concept)

### Minimum Viable Product
- **3 Flagship Eras** (Stone Age, Renaissance, Neo-Cyber)
- **1 Chronicle Quest per era** (3 total)
- **12 Core NPCs** (4 per era)
- **15 Side Mission templates**
- **Core real-time world clock**
- **Basic encounter system** (turn-based)
- **Settlement view** for one town per era
- **Offline hibernation system**

### Validation Goals
- Prove the real-time 1:1 loop is engaging
- Validate multi-day quest pacing
- Test check-in session flow
- Confirm players return day after day

### Then Scale
- Add remaining 67 eras
- Expand NPC roster
- Build Sigil saga arcs
- Implement collaboration features
- Add voice cloning integration
